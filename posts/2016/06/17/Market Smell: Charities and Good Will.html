Market Smell: Charities and Good Will

I think that business models relying on good will or charity are a market smell and where possible should be replaced with better business models. As economics or entrepreneurs we should not be content with a charity to solve a problem until we can honestly say we've exhausted every other possibility (or admit we are making a compromise in the name of pragmatism).

That's not to say I think we should get rid of existing charities, just that it's a symptom of an inefficient market. Wikipedia relies on donations instead of ads so that they can maintain their integrity. I agree that their integrity is important but so are the principles of capitalism. Relying on good will is a tax on kindness and generosity and doesn't incentivise the quality of information on wikipedia but rather their ability to plead to a possible donor's emotions.
